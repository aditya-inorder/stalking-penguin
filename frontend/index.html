How does this work?

Websites use browser fingerprinting to identify you. Instead of relying on cookies alone, they collect a unique set of traits from your device and browser, such as:

  • User agent & screen resolution
  • Color depth & available resolution
  • Installed plugins & available fonts
  • Timezone, language, & system language
  • Canvas fingerprint & local/session storage
  • Device DPI (XDPI/YDPI) & cookies

How to protect yourself?

Fingerprinting is hard to block completely, but you can reduce how much data is exposed. Stronger defenses include:

  • Use a VPN → hides your IP address and location.
  • Block trackers → tools like uBlock Origin stop many tracking scripts.
  • Choose privacy-focused browsers → some (like Tor or Brave) include anti-fingerprinting protections.
  • Test your fingerprint → sites like EFF Panopticlick or Am I Unique? show how identifiable your setup is.
